OUR STORY

Our paths first crossed as undergrads in the US, Anjali living an artistic life in Brooklyn, Jayant being an engineering nerd in upstate New York. We would meet occasionally, when Jayant was transiting through NYC or when the fellowship supporting their education would hold its annual conference. We became fast friends, but never more.

Conversations and meetings were few and far between after undergrad. Anjali hit the hustle of the design consultant world, chugging bad coffee, jet setting across the world. Jayant moved to the West Coast, still studying and also chugging coffee but the hipster, funky kind.

The fateful reconnection is a tale as old as the modern European summer fling. Jayant was living and working in Berlin when he got a message from Anjali who was planning a 3 month visit to Berlin to meet her colleagues that lived there. We spent most of that summer together, on a whirlwind adventure, full of train rides across Europe, Jayant ferrying Anjali on cargo bikes, and yes, a couple of romantic nights in Paris where we first kissed.

But the magical summer ended and gave way to two years of a long distance relationship as Anjali returned to NYC and Jayant continued his life in Berlin. We would find ways to see each other in-person every few months, including a magical couple of weeks in Goa - the inspiration for this wedding's location.

We were finally reunited when Jayant moved back to the US and we found our first apartment together - a sunlit, corner apartment, in a lovely little neighborhood in Brooklyn. Before long, we realized that we wanted to build a life together. Jayant first proposed to Anjali in Central Park, by the lake, a spot Anjali had painted recently. Anjali, not to be outdone, proposed back with a comic about our relationship, after a bike ride at the Storm King Art Center. Both proposals culminate in this wedding, a celebration with you, our family.
